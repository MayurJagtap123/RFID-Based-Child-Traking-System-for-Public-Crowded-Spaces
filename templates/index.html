Kumbh Mela

The Kumbh Mela is a major religious event in India, drawing millions of devotees to take part in sacred rituals along the banks of rivers like the Ganges. The event spans over several days and takes place in four locations: Allahabad (Prayagraj), Haridwar, Nashik, and Ujjain, with a vast congregation of people, including families with children. Due to the overwhelming crowd size, there is a significant risk of children becoming separated from their parents, making it essential to implement systems that can assist in quickly locating and reuniting lost children.

Child tracking systems using technologies like RFID tags, GPS wristbands, and mobile applications can greatly enhance child safety at Kumbh Mela. These systems can help authorities track children's movements in real time, send alerts to parents if their child is lost, and assist in locating them using a centralized database. The integration of AI-powered surveillance cameras, emergency alert systems, and public registration points ensures that children are quickly located and reunited with their families, making the Kumbh Mela experience safer for everyone involved.

Current Affairs in Kumbh Mela

Prime Minister's Participation

On February 5, 2025, Prime Minister Narendra Modi participated in the sacred rituals at the Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati rivers. His visit was marked by grand ceremonial events and prayers, and a boat ride to the Sangam with Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath.

Tragic Stampede

On January 29, 2025, a stampede occurred during the festival, resulting in at least 30 fatalities and 60 injuries. The incident took place during the pre-dawn hours as devotees surged towards the confluence of the three rivers, where the immersion is considered sacred. Witnesses reported chaotic scenes with people falling, being crushed, and belongings scattered on the ground.

Cultural and Spiritual Activities

The festival features various spiritual and cultural activities, including sacred baths on auspicious days, religious discourses, and cultural performances. Notably, on January 14, 2025, the first Amrit Snan (holy dip) took place, marking the beginning of the main bathing period
